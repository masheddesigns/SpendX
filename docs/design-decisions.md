# Design Decisions

## Why Local-First

SpendX is positioned as a privacy-friendly finance app. A local-first approach reduces onboarding friction, keeps the app useful offline, and makes it easier for users to trust the product with sensitive money data.

## Why Multiple Financial Domains in One App

Instead of tracking only expenses, SpendX brings together:

- transactions
- accounts
- cards
- goals
- salary
- lending and loans
- financial health

This reflects how users actually think about money: as one connected system rather than isolated tools.

## Why Analytics Are Built Into the Core Experience

The product is not only for logging data. The dashboards, summaries, and digests are meant to help users answer practical questions quickly:

- Where is my money going?
- Am I over budget?
- What do I need to review?
- Am I improving over time?

## Why Import Matters

Manual entry alone is not enough for retention in a finance app. Import, OCR assistance, duplicate detection, and merchant cleanup reduce effort and make the product feel substantially more useful.

## UX Direction

The interface is designed to feel:

- practical instead of gimmicky
- information-dense without looking intimidating
- personal and habit-forming through streaks, wrapped summaries, and progress cues

## Product Framing

SpendX should be read not just as an engineering project, but as a product design exercise in:

- financial behavior design
- trust and privacy
- reducing cognitive load
- balancing utility with delight
